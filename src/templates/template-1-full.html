STATEMENT OF WORK

This Statement of Work is issued under and subject to all of the terms and conditions of the Platform Partnership Agreement dated as of __________________, 2026, between Savant AI Technologies Inc. (“Company”) and _____ (“Platform Partner”).

1. Description of Services:

Platform Partner to provide general advisory services and product feedback to the Company as requested from time to time by the Company.

2. Definitions

For purposes of this Statement of Work, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

"Attribution Period" means the period commencing on the date a Client first engages Platform Partner through the Platform and continuing for twenty-four (24) months following such date; provided, that if Platform Partner's services under this Agreement terminate for any reason, the Attribution Period with respect to all Clients shall immediately terminate as of the date of such termination.

"Client" means any business, individual, or other entity that (a) Platform Partner introduces to the Platform, (b) engages Platform Partner to perform legal services through the Platform, and (c) is approved by the Company in writing as a Client for purposes of this Agreement.

"Cumulative GMV" means the aggregate Gross Merchandise Value generated by Platform Partner from all Clients from the Effective Date through the applicable measurement date, calculated on a cumulative (non-resetting) basis.

"Gross Merchandise Value" or "GMV" means the total dollar amount of fees billed by Platform Partner to Clients through the Platform during the applicable measurement period, excluding (a) any amounts refunded or credited to Clients, (b) chargebacks or disputed amounts, (c) taxes, and (d) any bonuses or incentive payments made by the Company to Platform Partner.

"Launch Period" means the period commencing on the Effective Date and ending on the earlier of (a) twenty-four (24) months from the Effective Date, or (b) the date on which the Company delivers written notice to Platform Partner that the Launch Period has concluded.

"Monthly GMV" means the Gross Merchandise Value generated by Platform Partner during a single calendar month.

"Platform" means the Company's online legal services marketplace, including all related websites, applications, and systems through which Clients engage Platform Partners for legal services.

(b) Payment Terms. Each commission payment shall be made within thirty (30) days following the date on which Platform Partner first achieves the applicable cumulative GMV threshold, subject to the Company's receipt of corresponding Client payments. For purposes of determining threshold achievement, GMV shall be calculated based on amounts actually collected from Clients, not amounts billed.

(c) Minimum Service Period. Notwithstanding the foregoing, Platform Partner shall not be entitled to receive any commission payments unless and until Platform Partner has provided continuous services on the Platform for a minimum of three (3) months from the Effective Date (the "Minimum Service Period"). Any GMV thresholds achieved during the Minimum Service Period shall be recognized, but commission payments associated with such thresholds shall not be payable until completion of the Minimum Service Period. Upon completion of the Minimum Service Period, all commission payments for thresholds achieved during such period shall become payable in accordance with Section 2(b). If Platform Partner's services terminate prior to completion of the Minimum Service Period, Platform Partner shall forfeit all commission payments.

(d) Commission Clawback. If Platform Partner voluntarily terminates this Agreement or is terminated by the Company for Cause (as defined in the Company’s applicable stock plan) within six (6) months following the Effective Date, Platform Partner shall repay to the Company fifty percent (50%) of all commissions paid to Platform Partner during the three (3) months immediately preceding termination within thirty (30) days of the termination date.

(c) Referral Incentive. (e) Vested Shares. Advisory Shares that have vested prior to termination shall remain the property of Platform Partner, subject to any repurchase rights, transfer restrictions, and other terms set forth in the Company's equity incentive plan and any applicable stock restriction agreement.

4. Client Non-Solicitation and Platform Exclusivity

(a) Non-Solicitation of Platform Clients. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12) months following termination for any reason (the "Restricted Period"), Platform Partner shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, contact, or provide legal services to any Client outside of the Platform, except with the prior written consent of the Company. For the avoidance of doubt, this restriction applies to all Clients introduced by Platform Partner to the Platform, regardless of whether such Clients had a pre-existing relationship with Platform Partner prior to the Effective Date.

(b) Platform Exclusivity for Onboarded Clients. During the term of this Agreement, Platform Partner agrees to provide legal services to all Onboarded Clients exclusively through the Platform and shall not engage any Onboarded Client for legal services outside the Platform during the Attribution Period applicable to such Onboarded Client. “Onboarded Client” shall mean any client that is serviced by the Platform Partner on the Company’s platform.

(c) Acknowledgment. Platform Partner acknowledges that (i) the restrictions in this Section 4 are reasonable and necessary to protect the Company's legitimate business interests in developing and maintaining the Platform and its Onboarded Client relationships, (ii) Platform Partner has received good and valuable consideration for agreeing to such restrictions, including the commission and equity compensation set forth herein, and (iii) the restrictions will not prevent Platform Partner from earning a livelihood.

(d) Remedies. Platform Partner acknowledges that a breach of this Section 4 would cause irreparable harm to the Company for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, the Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief to enforce the provisions of this Section 4 without the necessity of posting a bond or other security.

5. Modification of Compensation Structure

(a) Transition to Standard Platform Fee. The parties acknowledge that the compensation structure set forth in Sections 2 and 3 is intended to incentivize early adoption of the Platform during the Launch Period. Upon expiration or termination of the Launch Period, the Company may, in its sole discretion, transition Platform Partner to the Company's then-current standard platform fee structure by providing Platform Partner with not less than thirty (30) days' prior written notice (a "Transition Notice").

(b) Effect of Transition. Following delivery of a Transition Notice: (i) commissions earned prior to the effective date of transition shall remain payable in accordance with Section 2; (ii) Advisory Shares vested prior to the effective date of transition shall remain vested and shall not be affected; (iii) unvested Advisory Shares shall continue to vest based on Cumulative GMV achieved prior to the effective date of transition, but no additional vesting shall occur thereafter; and (iv) commencing on the effective date of transition, Platform Partner's compensation shall be governed exclusively by the Company's standard platform fee structure as communicated in the Transition Notice.

(c) Non-Ownership of Legal Fees . Client charges for legal services are charges of the Platform Partner alone, and all legal fees are earned by, owed to, and payable for the benefit of the Platform Partner, not the Company. As part of the Platform, the Company may provide invoice generation, billing workflow tools, payment-status tracking, and integrations with one or more third-party payment processors, including Stripe ACH, solely to assist Platform Partner in administering billing and collection workflows. In connection with such services, the Company acts solely as a technology provider and does not acquire any ownership interest in, or retain any portion or percentage of legal fees directly from the client. Any legal-fee funds processed through the Company and such payment integrations shall be routed solely for disbursement to accounts designated by Platform Partner, subject to Platform Partner’s sole responsibility for compliance with all applicable trust-account, safekeeping, and professional-conduct obligations. Pursuant to Section 5(a) above, the Company may charge Platform Partner separate client sourcing, software, administrative, or other platform fees (together, “Platform Fees”) only as expressly set forth in an applicable order form, pricing schedule, updated set of these Terms or other written agreement between the Company and Platform Partner. Such fees are compensation solely for access to and use of the Platform and related services and are not legal fees or a percentage of legal fees.

(d) Unilateral Modification Right. Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, the Company reserves the right to modify the commission rates set forth in Section 2(a) upon not less than sixty (60) days' prior written notice to Platform Partner; provided, that (i) any such modification shall apply only to Monthly GMV earned after the effective date of such modification, (ii) such modification shall not reduce any commission rate by more than twenty-five percent (25%) from the rates in effect as of the Effective Date, and (iii) Platform Partner may terminate this Agreement without penalty upon written notice delivered within thirty (30) days of receiving notice of such modification.

(e) Grandfathering. In the event of any modification to the compensation structure pursuant to this Section 5, Platform Partner shall be entitled to continue receiving commissions at the rates in effect immediately prior to such modification for a period of six (6) months following the effective date of such modification with respect to Clients onboarded prior to the effective date of such modification (the "Grandfathering Period"). Following expiration of the Grandfathering Period, all commissions shall be calculated in accordance with the modified compensation structure.

DEFEND TRADE SECRETS ACT, 18 U.S. CODE § 1833 NOTICE:

 

18 U.S. Code Section 1833 provides as follows:

Immunity From Liability For Confidential Disclosure Of A Trade Secret To The Government Or In A Court Filing.   An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any Federal or State trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that (A) is made, (i) in confidence to a Federal, State, or local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney; and (ii) solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (B) is made in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal.

Use of Trade Secret Information in Anti-Retaliation Lawsuit.   An individual who files a lawsuit for retaliation by an employer for reporting a suspected violation of law may disclose the trade secret to the attorney of the individual and use the trade secret information in the court proceeding, if the individual (A) files any document containing the trade secret under seal; and (B) does not disclose the trade secret, except pursuant to court order.
